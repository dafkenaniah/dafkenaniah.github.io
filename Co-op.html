The Lead's Guide to Co-op & H2H Stability

A playbook for managing the unique challenges of core online multiplayer modes.

Mastering the H2H/Co-op Test Hour

Dedicated, scheduled playtests are a primary method for finding stability issues. Your role is to organize these sessions and ensure the data gathered is clean and actionable.

  • Schedule Consistently: Regular sessions (e.g., 'hitting co-op again 4:45-7 today') are crucial. This creates a predictable rhythm for both QA and developers who often join to observe.
  • Use a Live Tracker: Maintain a shared Confluence page for each session to track games played, successes, and failures in real-time. This provides immediate visibility to production on the build's stability.
  • Capture All Perspectives: For any hang or assert, it is mandatory to get recaps from ALL players involved (Pitcher, Fielder, Batter, Observer). This is essential for debugging desyncs and network issues.

Deconstructing Hangs & Asserts

Online multiplayer bugs are complex. Your team must become experts at identifying the root cause and providing precise data.

  • Learn the 'Parse Method': Train your team to use the TTY parse on recaps to identify the exact state each user is in during a hang (e.g., 'GAMEWAIT_PITCH', 'POST PLAY'). This is a non-negotiable skill for writing useful online bugs.
  • Isolate Gameplay vs. Network: Many issues that seem like network hangs are actually gameplay logic bugs. The 'foul ball hang' was reproduced in Online Rated, proving it wasn't exclusive to Co-op and was likely a core gameplay issue.
  • Investigate Gameplay Mechanics: Deep gameplay knowledge is crucial. The investigation into Pinpoint PAR (Perfect Accuracy Region) size and pitches landing outside of it was a high-value find that directly impacted competitive balance.

Case Study: The Accidental Recap Hang

A hang was reported, but a developer (Connor) identified that it was caused by a user accidentally triggering a recap mid-play. He then taught the QA team how to identify this by looking for multiple 'Socket connected' entries in the TTY log. This is a perfect example of collaborative debugging that improves the entire team's skills and prevents invalid bugs from being escalated.
