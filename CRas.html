CRAS Lead's Guide to Project Management

A playbook for navigating the unique challenges of the Create-A-Stadium feature.

Managing Scope & 'Do Not Bug' Lists

CRAS is a complex mode with many interconnected systems. It's crucial to maintain a clear 'Do Not Bug' list to focus the team's efforts on what is currently testable and avoid wasted time on known, work-in-progress features.

  • Temporary & Placeholder Props: Regularly communicate with production about props that are temporary. Instruct the team to disregard props with filenames starting with 'ax' (Amateur Experience) or 'MS2' (Mini Seasons), as these are often slated for removal from the main CRAS editor.
  • Work-in-Progress UI: Clearly define which UI elements are not ready for testing. Early in the cycle, the Stadiumprops UI, Inventory UI, and the Tutorial were all marked as not buggable.
  • Server-Side Fixes: Be aware that some issues, like restricting CRAS stadiums in competitive H2H modes, will be handled by server-side changes. This means the client build may not reflect the final behavior, so coordinate with the online team before extensive testing.

Managing Vault & Year-to-Year Content

The CRAS Vault is a persistent feature, leading to complex year-to-year (Y2Y) bugs and community-driven exploits. This requires a dedicated testing strategy.

  • Prioritize Vault Testing: Regularly schedule heavy vault testing, especially after fixes are checked in for issues like CB-9856. This should include uploading from previous titles (MLB24) and downloading into the current one (MLB25).
  • Monitor Community Exploits: Keep an eye on community forums (like Reddit) for exploits. The issue where users could break foul territory parameters (CB-11579) was found this way. Encourage creative testing, like using a second controller to simulate stick drift, to reproduce these complex bugs.
  • Verify Competitive Safe Flags: The logic for what makes a stadium 'competitive safe' is a recurring source of bugs. Testers found that re-uploading a non-safe stadium could bypass the check. This area requires constant regression testing.

Case Study: The Y2Y Vault Crisis

A major issue arose where stadiums uploaded from MLB24 would not load correctly in MLB25 (CB-9856). The lead's response was to create a specific task force: QA was instructed to upload stadiums from '24, label them clearly, and then attempt to download and use them in '25 on the next patch. This structured approach allowed for a clear validation of the fix.
